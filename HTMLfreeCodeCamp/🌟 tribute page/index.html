FSK Satellite

Trap Kings

This is a timeline of FSK Satellite, from their creation to their breakup and the subsequent solo projects:

  • 2017 - FSK Satellite is formed in Venosa, province of Potenza, and was made up of Sapobully (initially known as SapoHaze), Chiello, Taxi B (previously known as Disa and later as TAXMANIA), ThugNizü, Powv, and YoungGucci
  • June 2, 2017 - Release of their first song Flexioni
  • September 21, 2018 - They released 'Zingaro', an independent album consisting of 8 tracks, with drill influences from Sapobully and emo influences from Chiello
  • January 2019 - The group's breakthrough began with the track 'FSK no spie' and later 'La prova del cuoco', which together reached 12M streams
  • July 12, 2019 - The real success began: they released their first album FSK 'Trapshit' under the Thaurus label and distributed by Universal. The album, belonging to the trap genre, featured influences of hardstyle, screamo, and emo
  • December 6, 2019 - The reedition of FSK Trapshit was released: FSK Trapshit Revenge, featuring 7 unreleased tracks including 'Capi della trap', in collaboration with Gué Pequeno. Thanks to this, FSK Trapshit reached sixth place in the FIMI Album Chart
  • January 2020 - FSK Trapshit Revenge received a gold certification from the Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana (FIMI)
  • Early 2020 - The various members of the group released solo singles: 'Crema di buccia' by Chiello, '10 sprite' by Taxi B, and 'Mitra' by Sapobully, all produced by Greg Willen, the collective's longtime beatmaker.
  • September 2020 - FSK Trapshit Revenge was certified platinum, and the group released their second album, "Padre Figlio e Spirito. Composed of fifteen tracks, it featured collaborations with rappers Sfera Ebbasta, Chief Keef, and Tadoe
  • June 29, 2021 - The members of the group announced via a statement on their social profiles their decision to split and focus on solo projects. On the occasion of the breakup, on July 1, 2021, the reedition of Padre Figlio e Spirito was digitally released, featuring the additional track 'Sudditaliano', once again produced by Greg Willen
  • October 14, 2021 - Chiello released his first solo album, Oceano Paradiso
  • 2022 - After a period of silence, Sapobully released a solo track, Too Famous
  • 2025 - The collective, now officially disbanded, continues their journey as solo artists. Sapobully has released a new album featuring friends on some tracks, while also undergoing a rehabilitation program in a hospital clinic
